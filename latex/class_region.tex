\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
Regions represent any rectangular space inside a \mbox{\hyperlink{class_dock_layout}{Dock\+Layout}}. Normally there is no reason to deal with Regions, unless you want to build complex docking systems or manually dock widgets into your code.

Regions are rectangular areas of the \mbox{\hyperlink{class_dock_layout}{Dock\+Layout}}\textquotesingle{}s contents\+Rect() which can be either subdiveded into sub\+Regions (all in a given {\bfseries{subdivision}} {\bfseries{direction}}) or contain a \mbox{\hyperlink{class_dock_widget}{Dock\+Widget}}. Every sub\+Region, if present, has opposite subdivision direction with respect to parent one. In addition, regions possess lists of separators and placeholders found along its subdivision direction. \mbox{\hyperlink{class_region}{Region}} information is owned by the \mbox{\hyperlink{class_dock_layout}{Dock\+Layout}} who allocates it; however, they are read-\/only accessible by the user.
