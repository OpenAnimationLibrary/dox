Detailed Description

Regions represent any rectangular space inside a DockLayout. Normally there is no reason to deal with Regions, unless you want to build complex docking systems or manually dock widgets into your code.

Regions are rectangular areas of the DockLayout's contentsRect() which can be either subdiveded into subRegions (all in a given subdivision direction) or contain a DockWidget. Every subRegion, if present, has opposite subdivision direction with respect to parent one. In addition, regions possess lists of separators and placeholders found along its subdivision direction. Region information is owned by the DockLayout who allocates it; however, they are read-only accessible by the user.

See also
DockLayout, DockWidget, DockSeparator and DockPlaceholder classes.
